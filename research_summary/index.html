Consistent Video Scene Exploration

Research Proposal - Jia Li

Overview

Given a single image or text prompt, consistent video scene generation aims to support long-term scene exploration under camera or user control, where users can freely navigate within a generated scene and revisit previously observed regions with consistent appearance and geometry. This problem is critical for applications such as immersive exploration using video generation models, similar to interactive gaming experiences, yet remains challenging for existing methods.

Research Problems

Although there are many works trying to achieve consistent video scene generation by maintaining a global 3D representation (e.g., point clouds or 3DGS), they often encounter scale ambiguity in depth estimation and introduce reconstruction artifacts that may further degrade video generation quality. In contrast, our core idea is to avoid these artifacts as much as possible and leverage accurate information to implicitly guide the video generation process.

1. Lack of Global Planning and Memory in Long-term Video Scene Generation

Existing long video generation models typically lack a global planning mechanism, resulting in incomplete scene structures, as shown in Video 1a. Even with memory mechanisms, they struggle to consistently return to previously generated states, as shown in Video 1b.

Video 1b. Inconsistency when returning to previously generated scenes. (generated from Matrix-Game-3.0)

To overcome this, we propose to introduce panorama as a planner instead of directly generating panoramic videos, which is computationally expensive and inefficient. Our preliminary ideas are:

  • Panoramas are generated only when the system detects key points to provide a global scene structure.
  • Intermediate frames are generated with smooth transitions when the player moves between key points.

Key Challenge: How to retrieve and align relevant panoramic information as conditioning signals for the video diffusion model, especially when players move across large distances in the scene:

2. Limited Generalization under Large-Range Camera Motion

Current video diffusion models often fail under large-range camera motions. For example, they struggle to rotate around an object (e.g., > 180°) to reveal unseen viewpoints, leading to inconsistent or unrealistic generations, as shown below:

Video 3. Large-range camera motion leads to inconsistent video generation (generated from Gen3R).

To address this, there are two potential approaches:

  • Extract structural guidance (e.g., depth or semantic maps) as conditioning signals to enhance the model's understanding of scene geometry and improve generation quality.
  • Find implicit geometric correspondences between source and target views in recent NVS models (e.g., LargeNVS), and inject them into video diffusion models using mechanisms similar to positional encoding.

Available open-source training datasets:

  • Real-world Datasets: DL3DV-10K, Realestate10K, etc.
  • Game-style Datasets: Context-as-Memory, etc.

Expected Outcome

With global planning, memory mechanisms, and geometry-enhanced video generation models, we expect to achieve long-term consistent video scene generation that allows players to explore scenes with large-range camera motions and revisit previously observed regions with consistent appearance and geometry. This will significantly enhance the immersive experience in games and other applications that rely on video generation models.
